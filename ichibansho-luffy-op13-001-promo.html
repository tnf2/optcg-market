Ichibansho Luffy OP13-001 Promo Card: U.S. Figure Release, Price & Scarcity

Published May 22, 2026 · By OPTCG Market

The Ichibansho Luffy OP13-001 promo card is getting attention because it is tied to a U.S. Bandai figure release, not a normal booster or starter product. The card comes with the Bandai One Piece Ichibansho Monkey D. Luffy figure, a 7.9-inch statue styled after the famous “Oda Signature” Luffy card artwork.

For a product that sat on preorder for almost a full year, the market only recently got the important confirmation: the U.S. release includes an exclusive trading card.

📋 Quick Facts

  • Product: Bandai One Piece Ichibansho Monkey D. Luffy Figure
  • Included Card: Luffy Promo Card
  • Card Number: OP13-001
  • Common Searches: Ichibansho Luffy Promo / Ichiban Kuji Luffy Promo
  • Release Date: May 25, 2026
  • Typical Retail: $75 USD
  • GameStop Price: $99 USD
  • Japanese Counterpart: Around $50 at time of writing

Why This Promo Matters

At first, many collectors thought the U.S. product was simply the Luffy statue. That made sense: the figure itself was the main listing, and the statue resembles one of the most recognizable Luffy card artworks in the game.

GameStop was the first major U.S. retailer to clearly confirm the figure included a promo card. That changed the conversation. A figure preorder became a card-market item overnight, especially once buyers realized the card number was OP13-001.

Retail Pricing and Early Sellouts

The standard U.S. retail price appears to be around $75, with retailers like Amazon and smaller hobby shops listing near that range. GameStop listed the same product at an elevated $99 retail price.

Even at the higher price, GameStop’s most recent preorder restock reportedly sold out in under an hour. Amazon sold out even faster. That is a strong signal for a product that many resellers may have ignored earlier because it looked like “just a statue.”

Japanese Version vs U.S. Version

The card also has a Japanese counterpart from a combo kit that included the statue, card, and additional items. That release began on February 14, 2026 and was not easy to secure, with some stores using raffle systems and paid tickets.

At the time of writing, the Japanese version of the card is trading around $50. The U.S. version is still early, but the setup is interesting: original preorders likely went to actual One Piece fans who wanted the figure, while later card-aware restocks were extremely limited.

Bottom Line

The Ichibansho Luffy OP13-001 promo has the ingredients collectors usually care about: Luffy, recognizable art, a bundled product, messy preorder information, and limited restocks. It is not guaranteed to run, but it is exactly the kind of release that can quietly become hard to source once figure buyers and card collectors start competing for the same supply.

If you are tracking One Piece promo cards, this is one to keep on the radar as the May 25 release date approaches.
