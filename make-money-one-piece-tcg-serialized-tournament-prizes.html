The Secret Best Way to Make Money in Trading Cards: Play the Right Game

Published May 22, 2026 · Updated May 25, 2026 · By OPTCG Market

The secret to making money in trading cards that almost no one tells new collectors is simple: play the right game.

Investing in sealed product and singles can be highly lucrative, but the bigger hidden edge is finding a game where competitive skill can unlock cards that normal collectors cannot pull from booster boxes. Right now, One Piece TCG is one of the clearest examples: high-end tournament prize cards, serialized cards, winner cards, and major event rewards can become serious secondary-market assets.

  • $300,000 BGS 10 Black Label English ST-01 “Championship” Luffy Serialized Card #651 of 700
  • $42,000 PSA 10 English OP12-020 Zoro Serialized Card #396 of 600
  • $4k–$5k Recent raw English serialized Yamato sales range

Why Playing the Right TCG Can Beat Pure Investing

There is a whole range of One Piece tournament prize cards that fetch serious money: Top 64 cards, winner cards, gold and silver prize cards, and serialized cards. Serialized cards are especially notorious because they have visible supply, unique numbering, major character demand, and a trophy-card feel that ordinary set cards rarely match.

Official events like Treasure Cup, Regionals, Championship Finals, and the World Final are where the real prize ecosystem starts to matter. Bandai’s official event pages describe Treasure Cup as a year-round tournament with special prizes, Regionals as events to decide the No. 1 player in a region, and World Final prizing as including serial-numbered trophy cards.

Why Other Big TCGs Do Not Have the Same Prize Edge

This is the key difference most people miss when they compare One Piece to the rest of the trading card market. Pokémon, Magic: The Gathering, Yu-Gi-Oh!, Lorcana, and Riftbound can all have valuable cards, promos, sealed products, and competitive scenes — but they generally do not have the same kind of high-value serialized tournament-prize ecosystem that One Piece has shown.

That does not mean those games are bad markets. Pokémon has unmatched brand power, MTG has deep collector history, Yu-Gi-Oh! has nostalgia and rarity spikes, Lorcana has Disney demand, and Riftbound may develop its own economy over time. The point is narrower: if the goal is to turn skill into access to rare, high-end prize cards, One Piece currently has a cleaner and more obvious path.

Market takeaway: One of the best ways to make money in trading cards is not only buying sealed product early — it is learning to play the TCG where skill can access tournament prize cards, serialized cards, winner cards, and better local card shop relationships.

Why it matters: prize cards cannot be pulled from normal booster boxes, and strong players can earn cards that collectors later chase on the secondary market. One Piece is the standout case because its prize ecosystem has produced headline-level results while still rewarding active players.

The max population depends on the card and region. For example, Championship Luffy has been tracked at 700 English, 77 Korean, 500 Japanese, and 248 Chinese / Asia copies — 1,525 total across those languages and regions.

The LCS Advantage Still Matters

Getting good at the right TCG is a win-win. In One Piece specifically, you give yourself a real shot at high-end tournament prizes, and you also become part of your local scene.

That matters because local card shops are often more willing to allocate sealed One Piece booster boxes, preorders, and fair-priced product to players they actually know. If you show up, learn, support events, and become a real part of the community, you are no longer just another online buyer competing for restocks.

Quick FAQ: Making Money in Trading Cards

What is the secret best way to make money in trading cards? Learn to play competitively in a card game where tournament prizes are scarce, collectible, and valuable. Right now, One Piece TCG is one of the clearest examples.

Which One Piece cards are valuable from tournaments? Valuable tournament-related cards include serialized cards, Top 64 cards, winner cards, gold and silver prize cards, and major championship rewards.

Why can playing beat pure investing? Playing gives you access to prize cards that cannot be pulled from normal booster boxes, plus better LCS relationships that can help with sealed product allocations, fair-priced preorders, and early market reads.

Do Pokémon, MTG, Yu-Gi-Oh!, Lorcana, and Riftbound have similar prizing? They can all have valuable promos, collectibles, and event items, but they generally do not offer the same level of high-value serialized tournament-prize upside that One Piece TCG has shown.

Bottom Line

If you are brand new and only thinking about sealed boxes, you may be missing the bigger opportunity. The best trading card edge is not always buying earlier than everyone else. Sometimes it is choosing the game with the strongest prize-card economy, learning it seriously, building LCS relationships, and putting yourself in position to win cards that money chases later.

That is the part most people do not tell you starting off.
