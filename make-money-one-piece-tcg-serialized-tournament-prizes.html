How to Make Money in One Piece TCG: Play the Game

Published May 22, 2026 · By OPTCG Market

The secret to making money in One Piece TCG that almost no one tells new collectors is simple: play the game.

Investing in sealed product and singles can be highly lucrative, but some of the biggest money in One Piece Card Game comes from tournament prize cards that cannot be pulled from normal booster boxes. If you get good enough to place well, you can win cards that most collectors will only ever chase on the secondary market.

💰 Reported High-End Serialized Card Sales

  • $440,420 Korean ST-01 “Championship” Luffy Top Prize Serialized Card #1 of 77
  • $300,000 BGS 10 Black Label English ST-01 “Championship” Luffy Serialized Card #651 of 700
  • $42,000 PSA 10 English OP12-020 Zoro Serialized Card #396 of 600
  • $4k–$5k Recent raw English serialized Yamato sales range

Why Playing Can Beat Pure Investing

There is a whole range of One Piece tournament prize cards that fetch serious money: Top 64 cards, winner cards, gold and silver prize cards, and serialized cards. Serialized cards are especially notorious because they have slightly more visible supply than some other top prizes while still feeling unique, numbered, and exciting.

Official events like Treasure Cup, Regionals, Championship Finals, and the World Final are where the real prize ecosystem starts to matter. Bandai’s official event pages describe Treasure Cup as a year-round tournament with special prizes, Regionals as events to decide the No. 1 player in a region, and World Final prizing as including serial-numbered trophy cards.

Serialized cards most commonly appear across English, Japanese, Korean, and Chinese / Asia regions, with cards awarded through tournament systems in their respective markets. The max population depends on the card and region. For example, Championship Luffy has been tracked at 700 English, 77 Korean, 500 Japanese, and 248 Chinese / Asia copies — 1,525 total across those languages and regions.

The LCS Advantage

Getting good at One Piece is a win-win. You give yourself a real shot at high-end tournament prizes, and you also become part of your local scene.

That matters because local card shops are often more willing to allocate sealed One Piece booster boxes, preorders, and fair-priced product to players they actually know. If you show up, learn, support events, and become a real part of the community, you are no longer just another online buyer competing for restocks.

🏴☠️ Learn the Market While You Play

OPTCG Market helps players and collectors track tournament prize cards, sealed product, restocks, and market movement so you can understand both sides of the game: playing and collecting.

Bottom Line

If you are brand new and only thinking about sealed boxes, you may be missing the bigger opportunity. The best One Piece TCG edge is not always buying earlier than everyone else. Sometimes it is learning the game, becoming good, building LCS relationships, and putting yourself in position to win cards that money chases later.

That is the part most people do not tell you starting off.
