Dodgers One Piece Night 2026 Promo Card Confirmed: DON!! Card, Ticket Prices & Dodgers Luffy Context

Published May 29, 2026 · Updated June 5, 2026 · By OPTCG Market

Update: the 2026 Dodgers One Piece Night promo card is now officially real. The Dodgers announced that fans attending the Thursday, July 2 game against the Padres can get a limited-edition One Piece card, while supplies last, alongside the co-branded One Piece Night hat.

This is exactly the kind of delayed confirmation collectors were watching for. When this article first went live, the only official giveaway was the hat and the card thesis was still based on last year's Dodgers Luffy rollout. Now the card has been unveiled, and the ticket market has reacted fast.

📋 Quick Facts

  • Event: Dodgers One Piece Night 2026
  • Date: Thursday, July 2, 2026
  • Game: Dodgers vs. Padres
  • Official Giveaways: One Piece Night Hat + limited-edition One Piece card
  • Card Status: Officially unveiled by the Dodgers
  • Card Type: DON!! card based on the revealed artwork
  • Distribution: While supplies last; likely first come first served if it follows the 2025 format
  • Ticket Market: Lowest available tickets have already moved to roughly $400+
  • Discord Edge: OPTCG Market Discord was alerted while tickets were still available under $200
  • Known 2025 Card: EB02-010 Monkey D. Luffy, commonly called Dodgers Luffy
  • Official Dodgers Post: Limited-edition card announcement
  • Official Dodgers Page: Dodgers Promotions & Giveaways
  • Ticket Link: Dodgers One Piece Night tickets

The Prediction Hit: The Card Is Official

The original market read was that Dodgers One Piece Night looked too similar to last year's setup to ignore. In 2025, the event initially looked like a stadium anime night with a straw hat giveaway. The card angle became obvious later, and early buyers had the cleanest shot at both access and resale upside.

That pattern has now repeated. The Dodgers' June 4 post says: “Join us for ONE PIECE Night on 7/2 and get this limited-edition card, while supplies last!” That moves the thesis from rumor to confirmed catalyst.

The important remaining unknown is distribution detail. The wording confirms limited supply, but collectors still need to watch whether the card is handed out at gates, at activation spaces, through special-event tickets, or through some combination of those mechanics. Last year, the card was distributed one per person, first come first served, while supplies lasted, at One Piece activation spaces inside Dodger Stadium.

Supply: Why “While Supplies Last” Matters

The phrase while supplies last is doing a lot of work here. If the 2026 card follows last year's pattern, not every person in the building should assume they are guaranteed one. Some market chatter is already modeling the drop as extremely competitive — potentially closer to one card for every few attendees depending on the final allocation and how distribution is handled.

That is why early arrival, event logistics, and ticket type matter. The card being official is only step one. The bigger edge may come from knowing where the card is distributed, when lines form, and whether the Dodgers or One Piece Card Game channels clarify quantities before game day.

Collector takeaway: the 2026 Dodgers One Piece card is confirmed, but the supply mechanics are still the market's biggest variable. “While supplies last” means the card should not be priced like a guaranteed-attendance freebie.

Best way to play it: if you already bought under $200 after the Discord alert, you are in a strong position. At $400+ entry, the risk/reward depends heavily on final supply, line logistics, and how the DON!! card is received compared to Dodgers Luffy.

Ticket Prices Are Already at $400+

Tickets were already firm while the card was still speculative. After the official reveal, the floor has moved again: the lowest available seats are now around $400 at the time of this update.

The key point for OPTCG Market readers is speed. The OPTCG Market Discord was notified about the likelihood of a card drop, and the ticket link was shared while available seats were still under $200. That is the entire edge in these stadium-promo trades: being early before the broader card market reprices the event.

From here, ticket prices are likely to be extremely sensitive to any official quantity update. If supply looks tighter than expected, $400 may not be the top. If the Dodgers later clarify a large allocation, the ceiling could compress, especially because this year's card design is not as obviously explosive as the original Dodgers Luffy.

DON!! Card vs. Dodgers Luffy: Less Exciting, Still High Value

The 2025 card had a near-perfect collector setup: a playable-looking Monkey D. Luffy card, Dodgers crossover artwork, and a surprise distribution window. That is why Dodgers Luffy became the reference point for this entire trade.

The 2026 card appears to be a DON!! card using the event collaboration artwork. That is probably less exciting than another character card or a true sequel to EB02-010. DON!! cards can still be highly collectible, but they usually do not carry the same character-driven demand as a Luffy promo with original baseball artwork.

That does not mean the card will be cheap. A limited official Dodgers x One Piece Card Game promo, distributed at a one-night stadium event, is still exactly the kind of item that can command a serious premium. The more realistic adjustment is that the ceiling may be lower than last year's Luffy card unless supply is meaningfully tighter than expected.

How Last Year's Dodgers Luffy Changed the Math

The original Dodgers Luffy card is one of the cleanest examples of why sports-stadium anime promos can become serious trading card assets. It had everything collectors like: official One Piece Card Game branding, special-event distribution, Dodgers crossover appeal, and original collaboration artwork.

The official 2025 announcement identified the card as EB02-010 Monkey D. Luffy. The card was tied to the July 3 Dodgers One Piece Night, while the straw hat giveaway was for the first 40,000 ticketed fans. The card itself was distributed separately, first come first served, while supplies lasted.

Today, the raw card is being watched around the $2,500 area, with prices moving quickly and graded copies pushing the market even harder. That is the backdrop behind this year's ticket rush.

Could the 2026 Dodgers One Piece DON!! Card Open at $500 Raw?

A $500 raw opening price is still realistic if the supply is tight and the card is hard to obtain on game day. The biggest support is not the design; it is the scarcity, the Dodgers x One Piece branding, and the fact that last year's event trained the market to chase this exact setup.

The bearish case is also obvious: this is a DON!! card with a less exciting design than Dodgers Luffy. If distribution is broad, or if too many people bought tickets purely to dump the card immediately, the market could settle below the most aggressive early targets.

The cleanest read is: lower ceiling than Dodgers Luffy, but still a high-value card if supply is genuinely limited and first come first served.

Keyword Data: What Collectors Are Actually Searching

In plain English: people were already searching for the card angle before the card became official. Now that the Dodgers have unveiled it, search demand and secondary-market attention should only accelerate.
