Round1 x One Piece Promo Cards: Registration, Dates & Collector Guide

Published July 1, 2026 · By OPTCG Market

The Round1 x ONE PIECE collaboration is not just another anime arcade event. For One Piece Card Game collectors, the important part is the new ONE PIECE CARD GAME -ROUND1- Promotion Pack: a 10-card promo set created specifically for the Round1 campaign.

That matters because the distribution is not a normal retail release. U.S. collectors have to register, be selected, visit a designated Round1 location during the pickup window, and reload a Round1 Game Card to receive cards. That is exactly the kind of friction that can make event promos messy early and interesting later.

What Are the Round1 One Piece Promo Cards?

The Round1 promo cards are a special One Piece Card Game collaboration pack tied to the 2026 Round1 x ONE PIECE event. According to the campaign announcement, the pack includes 10 original card designs made for the collaboration.

The bigger Round1 campaign includes arcade prizes, karaoke and food promotions, special game cards, exclusive merch, and themed experiences. But for OPTCG collectors, the card pack is the headline. It turns a general anime collab into a collectible card chase.

The strongest early search demand is still broad — DataForSEO shows "one piece promo card" and "one piece promo cards" at about 880 U.S. searches/month, while exact Round1 phrases are too new to show stable volume yet. That usually means one thing: the people searching today are early, and the cleaner Round1-specific content is still underbuilt.

How U.S. Collectors Get the Round1 One Piece Cards

For U.S. Phase 1, the process is not simply walking in and buying a sealed box. The current U.S. structure is:

  1. Register during the Phase 1 window: July 1 at 10:00 a.m. PT through July 5 at 10:00 p.m. PT.
  2. Wait for selection through the Round1 registration process.
  3. If selected, go to the designated Round1 location during the Phase 1 distribution period: July 18 through July 24, 2026.
  4. Reload a Round1 Game Card. The published U.S. terms say one trading card per $20 reload.
  5. Selected participants can receive up to 10 cards, subject to supply and the official rules.

Short answer: the Round1 x One Piece promo cards are not a normal booster product. In the U.S., Phase 1 requires advance registration, selection, in-person pickup, and qualifying Round1 Game Card reloads. That friction is why collectors should expect early market confusion around sealed packs, loose singles, full sets, and location-specific supply.

Why This Promo Could Matter More Than a Normal Giveaway

One Piece collectors have already seen how fast special promos can become their own market. Sports-night promos, figure promos, magazine promos, and event-only cards all behave differently from normal set cards because supply is tied to a behavior outside opening packs.

The Round1 cards have several traits that are worth watching:

  • Original artwork: the cards are described as original Round1 collaboration designs, not just a generic reprint announcement.
  • Registration friction: casual fans may miss the registration window entirely.
  • In-person friction: selected collectors still need to visit a participating Round1 location.
  • Random distribution: Round1 says participants cannot select, request, inspect, or exchange specific cards.
  • Set completion pressure: a 10-card set with random distribution can create demand for trades, singles, and sealed packs.
  • Time windows: Phase 1 pickup is only July 18 through July 24, with future phases expected later.

Market Read

This looks more like a collector/event promo chase than a simple arcade freebie. The safest market thesis is not "every card moons." It is that sealed packs, complete 10-card sets, and the most popular Straw Hat characters will likely become the first things buyers search for once real listings hit eBay, Discord, and TCGPlayer-style marketplaces.

What to Watch When Listings Start Showing Up

The early market will probably be messy. Some sellers will list individual cards without knowing the correct set naming. Others may list "Round1 One Piece card," "Round One One Piece promo," "One Piece Round1 pack," or just "OPCG Round1." That gives fast collectors a search edge.

When comps start appearing, pay attention to:

  • Sealed pack vs loose card premium: sealed usually carries extra trust and gambling value.
  • Full 10-card set pricing: completion friction may matter more than single-card scarcity at first.
  • Character spread: Luffy, Zoro, Nami, Sanji, Chopper, and other Straw Hat cards may not price evenly.
  • Condition: arcade/event distribution often creates more edge wear than pack-fresh hobby product.
  • Japanese vs U.S. supply: this is a simultaneous Japan/U.S. campaign, but distribution mechanics can differ by region.
  • Future phases: if later U.S. phases add supply, early prices may cool before stabilizing.

Should You Register?

If you are near a participating Round1 and can actually make the pickup window, registering is the obvious move. The downside is mostly time and planning. The upside is access to an event-exclusive One Piece Card Game promo product before the broader market has clean pricing.

If you are not near a Round1, the better move is to monitor early listings and Discord trade channels instead of panic-buying the first overpriced single. The first few days of a new promo are often dominated by bad titles, incomplete info, and sellers testing unrealistic prices.

Collector Warning

Do not assume every listing is complete or correctly described. Round1's terms say card selection is at Round1's discretion, so early sellers may not know how to identify the full 10-card checklist. If you are buying singles, ask for clear front/back photos and confirm whether the listing is one card, one sealed pack, or a full set.

Bottom Line

The Round1 x One Piece promo cards are worth watching because they combine a huge anime IP, original One Piece Card Game artwork, a 10-card completion chase, registration friction, random distribution, and in-person location limits.

That does not guarantee every card becomes expensive. But it does mean the first wave will probably reward collectors who understand the mechanics before casual buyers do. Register if you can actually pick up. If you cannot, monitor sealed packs, full sets, and cleanly photographed singles instead of chasing blind hype.
