Round1 x One Piece Promo Cards: Registration, Dates & Collector Guide

Published July 1, 2026 · By OPTCG Market

The Round1 x ONE PIECE collaboration is not just another anime arcade event. For One Piece Card Game collectors, the important part is the new ONE PIECE CARD GAME -ROUND1- Promotion Pack: a 10-card promo set created specifically for the Round1 campaign.

That matters because the distribution is not a normal retail release. U.S. collectors have to register, be selected, visit a designated Round1 location during the pickup window, and reload a Round1 Game Card to receive cards. That is exactly the kind of friction that can make event promos messy early and interesting later.

What Are the Round1 One Piece Promo Cards?

The Round1 promo cards are a special One Piece Card Game collaboration pack tied to the 2026 Round1 x ONE PIECE event. The set contains 10 Straw Hat crew character cards, with each character shown playing sports, arcade games, or other Round1-style activities. Early examples collectors are using to describe the set include Bowling Luffy and Baseball Zoro.

The bigger Round1 campaign includes arcade prizes, karaoke and food promotions, special game cards, exclusive merch, and themed experiences. But for OPTCG collectors, the cards are the headline. They turn a general anime collab into a collectible card chase, especially because the cards come as individually sealed one-card packs with one random character inside.

The strongest early search demand is still broad — DataForSEO shows "one piece promo card" and "one piece promo cards" at about 880 U.S. searches/month, while exact Round1 phrases are too new to show stable volume yet. That usually means one thing: the people searching today are early, and the cleaner Round1-specific content is still underbuilt.

How U.S. Collectors Get the Round1 One Piece Cards

For U.S. Phase 1, the process is not simply walking in and buying a sealed box. The current U.S. structure is:

  1. Round1 announced the collaboration early on July 1, 2026.
  2. Signups were scheduled to open later that day at 1:00 p.m. ET, with 9,000 signup slots available.
  3. The advertised signup window was supposed to run until July 6 at 1:00 a.m. ET, but demand was so heavy that signups closed within roughly 10 minutes.
  4. The site was crashing for many collectors during the rush, which likely slowed signups down; without the crashes, the 9,000 slots may have disappeared even faster.
  5. Successful signup holders can visit their designated Round1 location between July 18 and October 17, 2026.
  6. Each $20 reload to a Round1 play card earns one individually sealed random promo pack.
  7. Each signup can receive up to 10 packs, so maxing the promo requires $200 loaded onto a play card.

Short answer: the Round1 x One Piece promo cards are not a normal booster product. In the U.S., the 9,000 signup slots disappeared within about 10 minutes. Successful signup holders can get one sealed random one-card pack per $20 play-card reload, up to 10 packs, between July 18 and October 17. That friction is why collectors should expect early market confusion around sealed packs, loose singles, full sets, and location-specific supply.

Why This Promo Could Matter More Than a Normal Giveaway

One Piece collectors have already seen how fast special promos can become their own market. Sports-night promos, figure promos, magazine promos, and event-only cards all behave differently from normal set cards because supply is tied to a behavior outside opening packs.

The Round1 cards have several traits that are worth watching:

  • Original artwork: the cards are described as original Round1 collaboration designs, not just a generic reprint announcement.
  • Signup scarcity: 9,000 slots were gone within roughly 10 minutes, even with the site crashing for many users.
  • In-person friction: successful signup holders still need to visit their designated Round1 location.
  • Random sealed packs: each pack contains one of the 10 Straw Hat cards at random.
  • Set completion pressure: a 10-card set with blind distribution creates demand for trades, singles, sealed packs, and complete sets.
  • Spend requirement: the packs are free as a promo, but each one requires a $20 play-card reload; maxing 10 packs means loading $200.
  • Supply uncertainty: because Round1 likely did not expect every signup holder to max all 10 packs, total card supply may be below the theoretical 90,000 ceiling.

Market Read

This looks more like a collector/event promo chase than a simple arcade freebie. The safest market thesis is not "every card moons." It is that sealed one-card packs, complete 10-card sets, and the most popular Straw Hat characters will likely become the first things buyers search for once real listings hit eBay, Discord, and TCGPlayer-style marketplaces.

What to Watch When Listings Start Showing Up

The early market will probably be messy. Some sellers will list individual cards without knowing the correct set naming. Others may list "Round1 One Piece card," "Round One One Piece promo," "One Piece Round1 pack," or just "OPCG Round1." That gives fast collectors a search edge.

When comps start appearing, pay attention to:

  • Sealed pack vs loose card premium: sealed usually carries extra trust and gambling value.
  • Full 10-card set pricing: completion friction may matter more than single-card scarcity at first.
  • Character spread: Luffy, Zoro, Nami, Sanji, Chopper, and other Straw Hat cards may not price evenly.
  • Condition: arcade/event distribution often creates more edge wear than pack-fresh hobby product.
  • Japanese vs U.S. supply: this is a simultaneous Japan/U.S. campaign, but distribution mechanics can differ by region.
  • Future phases: if later U.S. phases add supply, early prices may cool before stabilizing.

Should You Register?

If you secured a signup and can actually make the pickup window, going earlier is the obvious move. The promo is listed as while supplies last, and the theoretical maximum is 90,000 cards only if all 9,000 signup holders receive all 10 packs. Real supply may be lower.

If you missed the signup or are not near your selected location, the better move is to monitor early listings and Discord trade channels instead of panic-buying the first overpriced single. The first few days of a new promo are often dominated by bad titles, incomplete info, and sellers testing unrealistic prices.

Collector Warning

Do not assume every listing is complete or correctly described. These are individually sealed one-card packs, so early sellers may not know how to identify the full 10-card checklist cleanly. If you are buying, ask for clear front/back photos and confirm whether the listing is one loose card, one sealed pack, multiple sealed packs, or a full 10-card set.

Bottom Line

The Round1 x One Piece promo cards are worth watching because they combine a huge anime IP, original One Piece Card Game artwork, a 10-card completion chase, a 9,000-slot signup rush, individually sealed random one-card packs, a $20 reload mechanic, and in-person location limits.

If you secured a signup, go sooner rather than later. If you missed it, monitor sealed packs, full sets, and cleanly photographed singles instead of chasing blind hype.
