Cotton Candy Chopper: One Piece Chopper Vol. 1 Promo Card, Price, Restocks & Where to Buy

Published April 23, 2026 · By OPTCG Market

Cotton Candy Chopper is the nickname collectors have given to the One Piece Chopper Vol. 1 JP promo card, numbered EB02-003. The card, which shows Chopper in a cartoon style eating cotton candy, comes packaged with One Piece: Chopper’s Magazine Vol. 1, a 22-page release that has become a real collector target.

Part of the appeal is simple: strong character demand, memorable art, and a promo that was cheap at retail but awkward to secure outside Japan. Add in confirmed foil treatment and messy restocks, and it is easy to see why the market has held up.

📋 Quick Facts

  • Product: One Piece: Chopper’s Magazine Vol. 1
  • Length: 22 pages
  • Included Card: Chopper Vol. 1 JP Promo
  • Card Number: EB02-003
  • Nickname: Cotton Candy Chopper
  • MSRP: $9.66 on Amazon Japan
  • Foil: Confirmed through in-person images
  • Market Range: Roughly $40 to $60 on eBay for the card and magazine

Why Cotton Candy Chopper Is Getting Attention

This is the kind of One Piece promo that checks a lot of boxes for collectors. Chopper is already one of the most popular characters in the franchise, and the artwork here gives the card instant identity. People are not just searching for the card number, they are remembering it as Cotton Candy Chopper, which says a lot about how sticky the release has become.

The card also has confirmed foil, which makes it feel more premium than a basic insert promo. That matters when a release is aimed more at collectors than pure competitive players.

How It Was Sold

For buyers in the United States, the cleanest MSRP route was Amazon Japan, where the magazine was priced around $9.66. That was the best-value path, but it did not stay easy for long.

The product originally sold out in January 2026. A restock landed on April 10, 2026, but a number of those orders were later cancelled due to insufficient inventory. Amazon Japan also limited purchases to 3 units per account, and some buyers who ordered in both the first and second wave reportedly got cancelled entirely.

It also appeared through Japanese retailers like Rakuten, Mangazenkan, and Kinokuniya, but those routes were less practical for overseas buyers and now appear to be out of stock. There was also in-store availability at Mugiwara Store at MAGNET in Shibuya.

Current Price and Market Read

Recent eBay sales for the card and magazine have been fairly steady in the $40 to $60 range. That is a meaningful jump from MSRP, especially for a product that looked inexpensive and accessible at first glance.

That does not mean this is a guaranteed breakout investment, but it does show real collector demand. The combination of a beloved character, recognizable art, confirmed foil, and unreliable primary-market access gave this promo a stronger setup than a lot of forgettable magazine inserts.

Bottom Line

Cotton Candy Chopper is a good example of how a low-cost One Piece promo can become a genuine collector target. At retail, it was just a small Chopper magazine with a bonus card. In the market, it has turned into a memorable promo with solid resale support and enough scarcity friction to keep buyers interested.
